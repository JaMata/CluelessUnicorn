Clueless Unicorns

Clueless Unicorns is a simplified version of the popular board game Clue®. The requirements of the application were set forth by the professors of Foundations of Software Engineering at Johns Hopkins University. In fact, this online-murder-mystery board game is the final project of the aforementioned course! We've moved through distinct software development lifecycle phases, providing deliverables all along the way. Until...we made it here. "We?", you ask?

Help

Matchmaking

Click the Start Game button on the welcome page to join the matchmaking queue! You will need two more players to start a match of Clueless Unicorns. Pro tip: Call two to five of your favorite pals to hop in the game with you for quick queue times and friendly competition.

Gameplay

Clueless Unicorns, similar to Clue®, is a turn-based board game. Accordingly, most of the actions you may take during the game can only be performed during your turn.

Movement

Move your unicorn with the ← , ↑ , → , and ↓ keys.

If starting your turn in a room, you may move into one of the adjacent rooms.

If starting your turn in a hallway, you may:

  • move into an adjacent, empty hallway.
    • You will lose your turn if the adjacent hallways are occupied!
  • take one of the four secret passageways.
  • remain in the room.
    • Only possible when placed at the scene of the crime during a player suggestion.

Suggestion

Gather clues to solve the mystery using the suggest feature. When in a room, click the suggest button to select the suspect and weapon of interest. The suggested room is automatically set to your current location. Once you've made your suggestion, the other players must disprove it.

Disproval

If you are prompted to disprove a suggestion, be sure to select one of your cards from the drop down menu and submit.

Notebook

Write down notes at anytime during the game. Leverage your note-taking skills to solve the murder before anyone else!

Accusation

Make an accusation at any time during your turn. Just click the accusation button and select the suspect, weapon, and room from the drop-downs.

Defeat

You lose the game when:

  • you make an inaccurate accusation.
  • another player solves the mystery before you.

Victory

Solve the mystery and win the game with the right accusation!
